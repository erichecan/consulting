Across Ontario, BC, and Alberta, the problems are rarely unique. The same failures appear in different companies, with different systems, sold by different vendors.

過去幾年，我哋喺ERP項目嘅每個階段都被請入批發分銷企業——選型之前、實施途中，以及上線崩潰之後。無論係安省、卑詩省定係亞伯達省，問題極少係獨一無二嘅。唔同公司、唔同系統、唔同供應商，出現嘅係相同嘅失敗。

These are not anecdotes. They are patterns.

The Common Thread: Misaligned Incentives共同主線：激勵機制不一致

ERP implementation partners are paid to deploy the software. They are not paid for what happens after. Their incentive is to reach go-live, close the project, and move on. Your incentive is for the business to work correctly on day one. These are not the same incentive.

ERP實施合作方係為部署軟件而收費嘅，唔係為之後嘅事負責。佢哋嘅動力係到達上線節點、結束項目、繼續下一個。而你嘅動力係業務喺第一日就能正常運行。呢兩種動力並唔一樣。

This misalignment is not a character flaw — it is a structural problem in how most ERP projects are contracted. The fix is not to find a better vendor. It is to have an independent party whose only job is to make sure your business is protected before, during, and after cutover.

呢種不一致唔係道德問題，而係大多數ERP項目合約結構上嘅根本缺陷。解決方法唔係搵一個更好嘅供應商，而係有一個獨立顧問，佢唯一嘅工作就係確保你嘅業務喺切換前、切換中同切換後都受到保護。

Why Upgrades Fail · 升級點解會失敗
Section 02 · 第二節

The Seven Root Causes of a Failed ERP UpgradeERP升級失敗嘅七個根本原因

When we conduct a post-mortem on a failed ERP project, we consistently find the same seven conditions. They are rarely present alone — usually four or five overlap, which is why the failure feels total rather than fixable.

每次我哋對一個失敗ERP項目做事後檢討，都會發現同樣嘅七個狀況。呢啲狀況極少單獨出現——通常係四至五個同時存在，所以失敗感覺係全面性嘅，而唔係某一個問題。

01
Hard cutover with no parallel-run period
硬切換，無平行運行期

The legacy system is decommissioned the moment the new system goes live. There is no period where both run simultaneously for validation. When something breaks — and something always breaks — there is nowhere to go.

新系統上線嘅瞬間，舊系統就被停用。冇任何時間讓兩套系統並行運行以作驗證。一旦出事——而出事係必然嘅——就完全冇後路。

02
Data migration not validated before cutover
數據遷移未在切換前完成驗證

Data is migrated and assumed to be correct. Customer accounts, product master records, and pricing configurations are not reconciled against the source system. Errors surface only under live order volumes.

數據遷移完成後被假設為正確，客戶帳戶、產品主記錄同定價配置均未與源系統核對。錯誤只係喺真實訂單量下先浮現出嚟。

03
"Soft knowledge" not captured before migration
「隱性知識」喺遷移前未被記錄

Per-client delivery exceptions, handshake credit terms, multilingual product names, and 15 years of account-level special arrangements exist only in staff memory and legacy spreadsheets. None of it makes it into the new system.

每個客戶嘅送貨例外、口頭信貸安排、多語言產品名稱，以及15年積累嘅帳戶級別特殊安排，只存在於員工記憶同舊Excel表格中，完全無法進入新系統。

04
Training done once, too early
培訓只做一次，且時機太早

Staff are trained weeks or months before go-live in a classroom setting. By the time the system is live, they have no hands-on recall. There is no refresher. There is no on-site support during the first week of operation.

員工喺上線幾週甚至幾個月前喺課室環境培訓。到系統真正上線時，佢哋早冇咗實際操作記憶，又冇補訓，上線第一週亦冇現場支援。

05
No rollback plan
無回退方案

No one defines what "failure" looks like, and no one documents what the business would do if the go-live does not succeed. When failure occurs, decisions are made in panic rather than from a prepared playbook.

冇人定義「失敗」係咩樣，亦冇人記錄上線唔成功嘅應對方案。失敗發生時，決策全靠恐慌驅動，而唔係按預先制定嘅應急方案行事。

06
Scope creep not caught before go-live
範圍蔓延喺上線前未被發現

Requirements added during the project are absorbed by the implementation team without formal scope changes or timeline adjustments. The project arrives at go-live date with configuration that has never been fully tested.

項目過程中新增嘅需求被實施團隊靜默吸收，冇正式更改範圍或調整工期。項目到達上線日期時，存在從未被完整測試嘅配置。

07
Go-live timed to vendor schedule, not business calendar
上線時間遷就供應商排期，而非企業業務週期

The cutover is scheduled when the implementation partner's project slots allow, not when it is safest for the business — often landing during peak order periods, month-end close, or key customer billing cycles.

切換時間按實施合作方嘅項目檔期安排，而唔係按對企業最安全嘅時機——往往落喺訂單高峰期、月底結算或重要客戶帳單週期。

Q Our ERP vendor says everything is on track. Do we really need an independent advisor?

Your vendor is paid to complete the implementation. Their definition of "on track" is whether they will hit their contractual go-live date — not whether your business will work correctly afterwards. An independent advisor has no stake in the vendor's timeline. Our only stake is your outcome.

你嘅供應商係為完成實施而收費。佢哋所說嘅「順利」係指能否按合約上線日期交貨——唔係你嘅業務之後係咪能正常運作。獨立顧問唔受供應商工期限制。我哋唯一嘅利益係你嘅結果。
